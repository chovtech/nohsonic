What Is Physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy (also called physical therapy) is a science-based healthcare profession that assesses, diagnoses, and treats conditions that affect movement and physical function. Physiotherapists use a combination of hands-on techniques, targeted exercises, education, and physical modalities like ultrasound and electrical stimulation to help patients recover from injury, manage chronic pain, and improve their overall quality of life.

Unlike simply resting and hoping pain goes away, physiotherapy addresses the root cause of your problem â€” whether that is muscle weakness, joint stiffness, nerve damage, or poor movement patterns. This evidence-based approach means faster, more lasting results.

  • Persistent back or neck pain â€” one of the most common reasons Nigerians visit a physiotherapist

  • Post-surgical rehabilitation after knee replacement, spinal surgery, or any major procedure
  • Neurological conditions such as stroke, Parkinson's disease, or multiple sclerosis
  • Work-related pain from sitting at a desk for long hours
  • Pelvic floor problems in women, especially after childbirth
  • Balance and dizziness problems (vestibular disorders)
  • Chronic conditions such as arthritis, fibromyalgia, or diabetes-related complications

What to Expect at Your First Physiotherapy Appointment in Abuja

Many people are nervous before their first physiotherapy session, especially if they have never been before. Here is a clear picture of what happens:

Initial Assessment: Your physiotherapist will spend time understanding your history, your symptoms, and how your condition affects your daily life. They will ask detailed questions and perform a physical assessment â€” checking your posture, range of movement, strength, and any areas of pain or restriction.

Diagnosis and Goal Setting: Based on the assessment, your physiotherapist will explain what they have found and what is causing your problem. Together, you will set realistic goals â€” whether that is returning to sport, going back to work, or simply walking without pain.

Treatment Begins: Some hands-on treatment often begins in the first session itself. This may include manual therapy, soft tissue work, therapeutic exercises, or the application of modalities such as TENS or ultrasound.

Home Exercise Programme: You will almost always be given specific exercises to do at home between sessions. These are critical to your recovery â€” physiotherapy works best when it is consistent, not just once a week in the clinic.
